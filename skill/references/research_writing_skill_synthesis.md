# Research Writing Skill Synthesis

This skill incorporates reusable writing principles from two external writing-skill repositories:

- `Master-cai/Research-Paper-Writing-Skills`: https://github.com/Master-cai/Research-Paper-Writing-Skills
- `Imbad0202/academic-research-skills`: https://github.com/Imbad0202/academic-research-skills

Use this file when polishing rhetorical logic, reviewer-facing clarity, or benchmark comparison.

## Adopted Principles

From `Research-Paper-Writing-Skills`:

- Clarify the paper story before sentence-level edits.
- Use reverse outlining: thesis, paragraph topic sentences, and evidence under each paragraph.
- Keep one paragraph for one message.
- Put the paragraph message in the first sentence.
- Connect sentences through cause, contrast, consequence, refinement, or example.
- Check claim-evidence alignment for Introduction claims.
- Avoid writing the work as a simple improvement over a naive baseline.

From `academic-research-skills`:

- Build writing from configuration, source corpus, outline, argument blueprint, draft, citation check, and review.
- Treat claim-evidence-reasoning chains as the backbone of the paper.
- Use peer-review style evaluation for originality, evidence sufficiency, argument coherence, and writing quality.
- Avoid throat-clearing openers, inflated adjectives, uniform paragraph lengths, synonym cycling, and mechanical three-point structure.
- Use sentence-length variation for Introduction prose: short turns for pressure, longer sentences for mechanism and evidence.

## Text-Mining Introduction Adaptation

For materials text-mining Introductions, these principles become:

1. Reverse outline from the material problem, not from the method.
2. One paragraph, one pressure step.
3. Topic sentence first; evidence and consequence after.
4. Every prior-method limitation must include the technical reason.
5. The method paragraph must explain why evidence-preserving schema/RAG/prompting is necessary.
6. The review pass should act like a skeptical Nature-family reviewer asking: "So what is the scientific bottleneck, and why does this method follow?"
